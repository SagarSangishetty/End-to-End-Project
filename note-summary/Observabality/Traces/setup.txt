Your app exports OTLP data to a fixed endpoint: http://otel-collector.observability.svc.cluster.local:4317 (from your ConfigMap). This name is hardcoded in your app config already — so whatever we deploy must create a Service named exactly otel-collector in the observability namespace, or traces silently go nowhere.
helm_release.otel_collector creates that Service (otel-collector) — this is the receiving end your app talks to. Its receivers.otlp block listens on 4317 for exactly this traffic.
helm_release.jaeger deploys Jaeger — and yes, in all-in-one mode it creates everything (ingest + storage + query UI) as one deployment, with its own Service name/port for receiving trace data.
The OTel Collector's exporters config is the glue — it needs to know Jaeger's Service name/port so it can forward what it receives from your apps onward to Jaeger. That's the otlp/jaeger.endpoint value I put as jaeger-collector:4317 — which is the part we haven't confirmed yet, since Jaeger 2.x's chart naming might differ from that guess.

So the direction is: App → otel-collector Service (4317) → OTel Collector processes/batches → forwards via its exporter config to → Jaeger's Service (whatever name/port that actually is) → stored in Badger → viewable via Jaeger's UI.
Your app never talks to Jaeger directly — it only ever knows about otel-collector. That's correct and is actually the standard production pattern (apps stay decoupled from the specific tracing backend; you could swap Jaeger for Tempo or anything else later by only changing the Collector's exporter config, not touching app code).
